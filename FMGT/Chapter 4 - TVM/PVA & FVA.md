## **Future Value of an Ordinary Annuity**
$$FVOA=PMT*\left[ \frac{(1+r)^{-n}-1}{r} \right]$$
PMT = The amount of each periodic payment

---
## **Present Value of an Ordinary Annuity**
$$PVOA=PMT*\left[ \frac{1-(1+r)^{-n}}{r} \right]$$
PMT = The amount of each periodic payment

---

**Question 1**
Compute the present value of a 10-year annuity that pays $1,000 annually, given a 10% discount rate. 
$$6144.57=1000*\left[ \frac{1-(1+0.1)^{-10}}{0.1} \right]$$

## **Present Value of an Annuity Due**
$$PVAD=PMT*\left[ \frac{1-(1+r)^{-n}}{r} \right]*(1+r)$$
PMT = The amount of each periodic payment

---


**Question 2**
Compute the present value of an annuity due of $1,000 per year for 10 years discounted at an annual rate of 15%.
$$5771.58=1000*\left[ 1-\frac{(1+0.15)^{-10}}{0.15} \right]*(1+0.15)$$

$$PVP=\frac{PMT}{r}$$
PMT = The amount of each periodic payment
